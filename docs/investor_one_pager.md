## The Problem

AI assistants like ChatGPT are powerful — but they're black boxes. Your conversations, files, and API keys live on someone else's server. And they're just chat windows: they can't actually *do* anything in your digital life.

Professionals and privacy-conscious users want an AI that **acts**, not just talks — without giving up their data.

---

## The Solution

**OZY2** is a local-first AI assistant that runs entirely on your computer and connects to 38+ real-world tools — Gmail, GitHub, Calendar, Notion, Slack, Smart Home, and more.

- ✅ **100% local** — no OZY2 account, no cloud storage, zero data exposure
- ✅ **Any AI provider** — bring your own key: Gemini, GPT-4o, Claude, or Ollama (fully offline)
- ✅ **Real tool use** — not just chat; reads your emails, manages tasks, controls devices
- ✅ **Native installer** — double-click to install on Mac, Windows, Linux. No terminal needed.

## Why Now

1. **AI adoption is mainstream** — ChatGPT hit 100M users in 60 days; demand for AI tools is structural, not cyclical
2. **Privacy backlash is real** — GDPR, data breaches, and AI scraping fears drive users toward local tools
3. **Open source has won** — VS Code, Linux, Postgres. Users trust code they can audit
4. **No dominant local-AI player** — The gap between "just a chat" and "full automation" is wide open
